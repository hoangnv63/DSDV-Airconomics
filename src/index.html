Airconomics

Exploring the relationship between air quality, population, and economic growth

Air pollution is not just an environmental issue. It is deeply connected to economic development, population growth, and global inequality. This project visualizes how PM2.5 air pollution has evolved across countries and over time, and how it correlates with GDP and population.

Through interactive charts and maps, users can explore global trends, compare individual countries against the world average, and observe long-term environmental patterns from 1990 to 2020.

  • Bubble chart comparing GDP, PM2.5, and population
  • Line chart tracking PM2.5 trends by country vs world average
  • Choropleth map visualizing global PM2.5 distribution by year

Dataset source: processed global environmental and economic indicators (PM2.5, GDP, population).
